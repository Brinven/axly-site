## Design Context

### Users
**Primary audience**: Developers, researchers, creators, AI enthusiasts — people who discover tools and build things. They browse with intent but enjoy exploring. They're technically literate and allergic to marketing fluff.

**Secondary audience (for /webdesign/)**: Non-technical small business owners in the Houston area looking for a website. They're evaluating whether to trust this person with their online presence. They need to feel confident quickly.

**Context**: Most visitors arrive via search, social, or direct link. They're in browse/evaluate mode — not rushed, but not patient with generic pages either. They want to understand what this is and whether it's worth their time within 5 seconds.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Bold. Curious. Crafted.

**Voice**: Direct, slightly irreverent, one person talking to you straight. "Like a smart friend who builds cool stuff and tells you about it." No VC-speak, no enterprise fluff, no buzzwords. Contractions are fine. First person singular ("I build").

**Emotional goals**:
1. Curiosity/discovery — "this is cool, I want to explore"
2. Credibility — "this person makes serious stuff, not a side project"

### Logo & Brand Colors
The Axly's Customs logo features chrome/metallic text with stylized wings transitioning from hot pink through purple to steel blue. It has cyberpunk energy crossed with a custom shop aesthetic — detailed, bold, unapologetic.

**Extracted palette** (approximate from logo, to be refined in CSS tokens):
- **Hot pink** (primary accent): `oklch(0.65 0.25 350)` — ~#D81B7F — the dominant warm tone in the wings and "AXLY'S" text
- **Steel blue** (secondary accent): `oklch(0.7 0.12 230)` — ~#4BA8D8 — the cool tone in wing tips and edges
- **Deep navy** (background): `oklch(0.15 0.04 280)` — ~#0F0B2E — the darkest tones at the wing base
- **Chrome/silver** (metallic neutral): `oklch(0.78 0.01 270)` — ~#B8B8C8 — the "CUSTOMS" text treatment
- **Purple** (transitional): `oklch(0.45 0.2 310)` — ~#7A2D8E — the bridge between pink and blue in the wings
- **Off-white** (text on dark): `oklch(0.95 0.005 280)` — ~#F0EFF4 — tinted toward the brand hue, not pure white

**Colors NOT in the brand**: Emerald green (the current accent), generic grays, pure black, pure white.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Dark-first. The logo's chrome and neon palette demands dark backgrounds. These colors die on white.

**Tone**: Industrial-creative. Think custom auto shop meets tech workshop. Not brutalist, not minimalist — this brand has *detail* and *craftsmanship*. The logo's metallic treatment and wing motifs set the bar: every element should feel intentionally made, not templated.

**What it IS**:
- Chrome accents and metallic touches that echo the logo
- Bold display typography with real presence
- Asymmetric layouts that break monotony
- High contrast moments — hot pink on deep navy hits hard
- Generous space that lets the design breathe
- Subtle motion at high-impact moments (hero, section entries)
- A sense of *workshop* — things being made, not just displayed

**What it is NOT**:
- Corporate/enterprise (no suits energy)
- Generic AI aesthetic (no purple-to-blue gradients, no cyan-on-dark, no glassmorphism)
- Cardocalypse (not everything in identical rounded rectangles)
- Minimal to the point of boring (the logo is bold — the site should match)
- Stock photo dependent (design-driven hero, not photo-driven)

**Anti-references**:
- Purple gradients on white backgrounds
- Inter/Roboto/Arial as display fonts
- Nested cards inside cards
- Every section as a 3-column grid
- Generic "hero + headline + two buttons + mockup" layout
- Syne, DM Sans, or any font from the reflex list

### Design Principles

1. **Workshop, not storefront** — This is a place where things are made. Show the work, not a sales pitch. Every section should feel like opening a different drawer in a well-organized workshop.

2. **Earn attention through craft, not volume** — One striking hero beats five mediocre sections. Use restraint in quantity, boldness in execution. Every element should justify its existence.

3. **Dark canvas, bright signal** — The deep navy background is the canvas. Hot pink and steel blue are signal colors used sparingly for maximum impact. Chrome/silver carries the body. Overusing the accents kills them.

4. **Asymmetry is confidence** — Centered, symmetrical layouts feel safe and generic. Offset elements, varied column widths, and unexpected compositions signal that a human designed this.

5. **Invite exploration, reward attention** — The site should make you want to scroll. Each section should feel different enough to hold interest, while the design language holds everything together.

### Key Design Decisions (confirmed)
- **Dark only** for launch. No light/dark toggle. The brand IS dark mode.
- **Nav bar**: Web Design | Goldmine | ThothAI | Art | Blog + "Get a Website" CTA button (hot pink)
- **Gallery on homepage**: Dynamic pull from images.json (rotating showcase, not hand-picked)
- **The Shovel newsletter**: Prominent standalone section — it's the funnel that makes everything else accessible. Full-width CTA strip, high contrast, subscribe action.
- **No emoji as design elements**: All replaced with custom graphics or SVG icons
- **Custom graphics**: Mike generates assets locally. Flag any spot that needs one.

### Accessibility
- WCAG AA compliance
- Reduced motion support via `prefers-reduced-motion`
- No other specific accommodations required
- Ensure hot pink on deep navy passes contrast checks (adjust lightness as needed)

### Graphics & Icons
- **No emoji as design elements.** All current emoji (🎨 art icon, ☀/☽ theme toggles, etc.) must be replaced with custom graphics or purposeful SVG icons.
- **Prefer custom images over icon libraries.** Mike has extensive local image generation capability. Any time a section would benefit from a custom illustration, icon set, or decorative graphic — flag it. Creating these assets showcases the brand's capabilities.
- **Asset request format**: When flagging a needed graphic, describe: subject, approximate dimensions, style direction (e.g., "chrome/metallic icon of a pickaxe, 64x64, transparent PNG or SVG").

### Technical Constraints
- Pure HTML/CSS/JS — no frameworks, no build tools
- Cloudflare Pages static hosting
- Google Fonts (loaded async, preconnected)
- All images WebP, lazy-loaded (except hero)
- Target: 90+ PageSpeed mobile
- CSS custom properties for all tokens — no hardcoded colors
